5b Eight Principles — Pattern Manifestations
Full Heat (Excess Yang) — External Yang (Wind-Heat, Summer-Heat) · Internally generated (Heart-Fire, Liver-Fire) · Transformation (Wind-Cold → interior Heat)
S&S: Restlessness, headache, pain worse with pressure, whole face red, red tongue + thick yellow coat, Full-Rapid-Overflowing pulse

Universal Differentiator: Pressure Response
Full/Excess → pain worse with pressure. Deficiency → pain better with pressure & warmth. Applies to BOTH Cold and Heat pairs.
5b+ Eight Principles — Relationships & Combinations

Key Definition: Interior vs Exterior
Differentiation is made on the basis of LOCATION of the disease — NOT the etiology (cause). A disease caused by an exterior factor that has penetrated to the Internal Organs = Interior pattern.

Two Types of Exterior Conditions:

  • Type 1: Affects skin & muscles from exterior pathogenic factor — acute onset (Wind-Cold, Wind-Heat)
  • Type 2: Affects channels — slower onset. Bi Syndrome / Painful Obstruction Syndrome — obstruction of Qi in channels by Cold, Dampness, Wind, or Heat

Cold↔Heat Relationships (Slides p.25):

  • Cold transforms to Heat: Cold occurs 1st → gradually changes into Heat. Example: Ext-Cold → fever, aversion to cold, aches → aversion to cold subsides, fever persists, irritability, thirst, yellow tongue
  • Heat transforms to Cold: Heat 1st → cold limbs, pallor, deep slow pulse appear suddenly

⚠ False Heat–True Cold vs Empty-Cold: NOT the same!
Empty-Cold/Heat arise from deficiency of Yin or Yang — there IS genuine Cold/Heat.
False conditions: the appearance is false — there is NO actual Heat or Cold.
⚠️ Collapse of Yin / Collapse of Yang — Most Dangerous Patterns
When Yin or Yang is so severely depleted it can no longer hold the other in check:
Collapse of Yang: Sudden profuse sweating, cold clammy limbs, very faint pulse, pale face — Yang fleeing outward
Collapse of Yin: Profuse sweating (hot and sticky), flushed face, rapid empty pulse, feeling of heat — Yin exhausted
Clinical note: These are emergency states — not the same as Yang def or Yin def, which are chronic. Collapse = acute, life-threatening.

Internal Wind vs External Wind (From Slides)
Internal Wind: Associated with Liver. Involuntary movements, rigidity, tremors, convulsions, stiffness, paralysis, deviated tongue, pain that moves. Think: inflatable car salesperson — irregular flapping = wind. Turn dial to 11 = stiffness/rigidity.
External Wind: Associated with Lungs. Rapid onset, rapid changes, affects skin and top of body, causes itching, floating pulse, aversion to cold/wind, occipital stiffness.
Miley Cyrus clinical pearl: yellow coat = Heat, deviated tongue = Internal Wind. Always check if mechanical (trauma) before diagnosing.

Other Factors Affecting Pulse
Time of day: 12 PM = Heart time → stronger Heart pulse (not pathology).
Emotions/activity: Running late → rapid. Coffee → rapid/full. Always ask before diagnosing.
Medications: Beta-blockers → slow/deep (cannot diagnose). Tranquilizers → sluggish/indescribable.
Constitution: Petite elderly woman may have a big strong pulse. Don't judge by appearance.
