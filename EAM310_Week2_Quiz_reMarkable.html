★ BONUS Short Answer

According to Maciocia (FCM Ch.25) and Prof. Schrier, how many finger movements are used when taking the pulse? Name all of them, describe the movement direction, and state what each detects.

Answer: 5 movements:
1. Lifting — lift upward → detects superficial level (Floating, Normal, Deficient at surface)
2. Pressing — press downward → detects deep level (Deep, Hollow, Hidden, Empty)
3. Searching — hold still → counts rate (Slow, Rapid, Normal)
4. Pushing — move side-to-side (lateral–medial) → detects shape (Slippery, Wiry, Tight, Choppy, Fine, etc.)
5. Rolling — move proximal–distal (back and forth) → detects length (Short, Long, Moving); also used for infant pulse under 1 year

Note: Maciocia's "Remember" box in FCM Ch.25 lists 4 (Lifting, Pressing, Pushing, Rolling — subsumes Searching). Know all 5.

Answer Key — No Peeking Until You've Tried Everything
1. B — Hearing and Smelling (聞 Wén)
These two are ONE pillar. The trap is counting them separately and arriving at five. The answer is always FOUR.
2. B is WRONG
Reflecting Yin organs and Blood describes the tongue BODY (體 / Ying level), not the Coat. The Coat reflects Yang organs, pathogenic factors, and digestive strength.
3. C — Empty Heat (Yin Deficiency)
Red tongue + peeled coat (no coating) + afternoon fever + night sweats + five-palm heat = Empty Heat constellation. Full Heat would have thick yellow coat + forceful pulse + whole face red.
4. B — Exterior pattern
Floating + fever + chills + stiff neck + runny nose = pathogen at the surface. Contrast with floating in Yin def: night sweats, five-palm heat, malar flush, dry throat at night — totally different constellation.
5. C — Eight Principles (八纲)
Eight Principles is the foundation — applied universally in every case regardless of tradition. All other systems build on it.
6. C is WRONG — Stone-like/Deep is WINTER
Autumn = Hair-like/Floating (毛 Máo). Winter = Stone-like/Deep (石 Shí). Spring = Wiry (弦). Summer = Flooding (洪). Classic trap — confusing Autumn with Winter.
7. D — Blood Stasis
Schrier's call-and-response: Blood Stasis = stabbing + purple. Fixed/boring pain, purple lips, dark clots, choppy pulse = textbook Blood Stasis. Qi stagnation = distension + moving pain.
8. FALSE
Hearing AND Smelling together = ONE pillar (聞 Wén). Total = FOUR, not five.
9. FALSE
Floating pulse also = Yin deficiency (night sweats, five-palm heat, malar flush, dry throat at night). Always look at the full constellation before concluding exterior.
10. TRUE
The tongue body color always shows the true condition — it reflects Yin organs, Blood, and Nutritive Qi at the Ying level. The tongue does not necessarily show the WHOLE condition, but the body color is the most reliable indicator.
11. FALSE
Full Cold = pain WORSE with pressure (excess resists). Empty Cold = pain BETTER with pressure and warmth (deficiency wants support). This is the universal Excess/Deficiency differentiator.
12. TRUE
Wiry pulse in spring = normal seasonal variation. Yang is arising, life is bursting forth — pulse is naturally more tense. In a healthy person, do NOT diagnose Liver disease based on seasonal wiry pulse alone.
13. FALSE
Pepto-Bismol black tongue = bismuth + oral sulfur. Completely harmless. Ask immediately — "Are you taking Pepto-Bismol?" Mystery solved, no pathology.
