🙈 Answer Key — No Peeking Until You've Tried

1. Four Pillars:
望 Wàng (Observation/Seeing) · 聞 Wén (Hearing & Smelling — ONE pillar) · 問 Wèn (Inquiry) · 切 Qiē (Palpation). Four not five because hearing and smelling are bundled as 聞.
2. Interrogation → Inquiry:
Schrier: "We're not the CIA." The goal is curiosity and openness — "Tell me more about how this shows up for you." The energy of the question shapes what the patient shares.
3. Three reasoning types:
Deductive (general rule → this patient: fever+chills+floating pulse → exterior). Inductive (specific observations → general rule: historically floating pulse = exterior). Analogical (A causes B, A predicts B: sustained Heat → damages Blood → internal Wind vector opens).
4. Floating pulse — two patterns:
EXTERIOR: fever + aversion to cold (simultaneously), stiff neck, occipital headache, runny nose. YIN DEFICIENCY: night sweats, five-palm heat, malar flush (cheeks not whole face), afternoon fever, dryness. The floating pulse in Yin def. is because deficient Yin cannot anchor Yang.
5. Cold hands, warm torso:
NOT automatically a Cold pattern. More likely Qi stagnation blocking warm Qi from reaching extremities. The core is hot — the Qi is simply stuck and not circulating outward. Context: look at tongue, full pulse picture, other signs.
6. Tongue zones:
Tip = Heart · Just behind tip = Lung · Sides = Liver/Gallbladder · Center oval = Spleen/Stomach · Root/Back = Kidney, Bladder, Intestines.
7. Three tongue layers:
Coat = most superficial, Yang organs & pathogenic factors. Body = Yin organs, Blood, Nutritive Qi (Ying level). Sublingual veins = deepest, Yuan (Original) Qi; Blood stasis if dark/distended/tortuous.
8. Tongue spirit:
How alive it looks — fresh, slightly moist, slightly lustrous. Reflects Stomach-Qi. Good spirit = good prognosis even in disease. Spiritless ("cow tongue at the deli" — dull, dry, gray) = serious Stomach-Qi depletion. Always note this.
9. Patient prep instructions:
(1) Don't brush/scrape tongue — "cleaning up a murder scene." (2) No perfume/cologne/essential oils — interfere with smelling diagnosis. (3) Poker face on examination — never say "it looks dead"; say "your chi is good" or "this is great information."
10. Red + coat vs Red + no coat:
Red + THICK YELLOW coat = Full-Heat (pathogen present; coating reflects Yang organs/Stomach's pathogen load). Red + NO coat = Empty-Heat from Yin deficiency (Yin consumed → Stomach-Yin failing to "steam" a coat upward).
11. Pulse positions:
LEFT: Cun=Heart/PC · Guan=Liver/GB · Chi=Kidney-Yin. RIGHT: Cun=Lung/LI · Guan=Spleen/Stomach · Chi=Kidney-Yang.
12. Ibn Sina:
Avicenna (980–1037 CE), Persian polymath of the Islamic Golden Age. Quote: "Only the musically trained can truly know the pulse. The pulse is of a musical nature." Means: pulse requires time and repetition to develop sensitivity, like learning an instrument.
13. Normal pulse:
Stomach-Qi = gentle, calm, unhurried (reflects digestive function). Spirit = soft but with strength, regular rhythm, "alive" (reflects Mind vitality). Root = clearly felt deep and at Chi/rear position (reflects Kidney Essence intact).
14. Journal protocol:
5 people minimum, each seen 4–5+ times. 50 total tongue + pulse observations. J1–25 due at Midterm (Aug 7). J26–50 due at Final (Sep 11). ~6–7 entries/week starting now.
C1: Pattern
Exterior Wind-Cold (Excess). Location: exterior (simultaneous fever + chills, floating pulse). Nature: Cold (no thirst, thin white coat, tight pulse — tight = Cold contracting). Degree: Excess (no sweating = pathogen blocking pores, forceful tight pulse).
C1c: Interior transmission
Pulse would become deep (沉). Tongue coat would thicken and likely turn yellow. Key clinical marker = aversion to cold disappears (it was there only while pathogen was at the surface).
C2: Full pattern
Floating pulse in Yin deficiency — NOT exterior (Schrier's clinical pearl). Tongue body = Yin organs/Ying level → red = Heat. NO coat = Yin deficiency (Stomach-Yin failing to steam up a coat). Pattern: Yin Deficiency with Empty-Heat. Interior · Heat · Deficiency. The floating thready rapid pulse confirms Empty-Heat not Full-Heat.
