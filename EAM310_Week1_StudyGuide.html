Eight Principles — Bā Gāng 八綱 八綱

Foundation for ALL other methods of pattern differentiation in TCM

System of differential diagnosis using four pairs of opposites (Yīn/Yáng, Cold/Hot, Deficient/Excess, Interior/Exterior). Rooted in Sūwèn & Shāng Hán Lùn (Han dynasty); term coined Qing dynasty (1644–1912), based on Zhāng Jǐngyuè's Jǐngyuè Quánshū — greatly simplified. Critical rule: Interior vs Exterior is determined by the LOCATION of the disease — NOT by its etiology (cause).

Exterior Treatment Principles

  • Expel the exogenous pathogen; keep evil from entering interior
  • Acupuncture: Open meridians; expel pathogens. Points: head, arm, upper back (Yáng areas). Cupping on shoulder/upper back.
  • Herbs: Light/leaf herbs float to exterior — Jīn Yín Huā, Bò Hé, Fáng Fēng, Xīn Yí Huā. "Lighter = works at surface" (law of signatures)

Interior Treatment Principles

  • Set treatment by real conditions (interior cold, heat, def, excess)
  • Acupuncture: Chest, back, leg, foot points; Mù points; Back-Shū points; Lower-He points; Source points
  • Herbs: Roots, seeds — "heavy" medicinals that sink to the interior

Disease Transmission — Inward vs Outward Progression

Inward (worse): Aversion to cold disappears → feeling/aversion to heat begins. Caused by: weakened body resistance; hyperactivity of pathogens; improper/delayed treatment.

Outward (improvement): Pulse becomes less deep; tongue coat thins. Caused by: correct treatment, good care, strengthened resistance.

Full / Excess — Empty / Deficient (實 — 虛) 實虛

Core Concept — Relative Strength of Pathogens vs Righteous Qì (Zhèng Qì)

Full = presence of a pathogenic factor (NOT excess of Zhèng Qì). Empty = deficiency of Zhèng Qì (includes Qì, Blood, Yīn, Yáng deficiency). One of the most important TCM concepts due to the emphasis on balance.

A+ Exam Traps — Golden Rules
RuleDetail
Interior vs Exterior = LOCATION not causeAn exterior pathogen causing interior organ symptoms = Interior pattern. The etiology doesn't determine the category.
Full ≠ excess Zhèng QìFull means a pathogenic factor is PRESENT. Empty means Zhèng Qì is DEFICIENT.
False Heat/Cold ≠ Empty Heat/ColdFalse = appearance is deceptive, no actual Heat/Cold. Empty = real but relative Heat/Cold from Yīn/Yáng deficiency.
Pre-Heaven Essence = fixedCANNOT be replenished. Only Post-Heaven Essence can top it up. You get one candle — preserve it.
Wèi Qì = 50×/24h25× during day, 25× at night. Circulates OUTSIDE vessels in the Còu Lǐ. Linked to Lungs.
Yíng Qì = acupuncture QìThe Qì activated during acupuncture needling. Circulates INSIDE vessels with Blood. Yīn aspect of Zhēn Qì.
Qì commands Blood; Blood mothers QìQì moves, generates, holds Blood. Blood nourishes organs that produce Qì. Treat both in chronic cases.
Phlegm root = SpleenPrimary cause always. "Spleen is the source; Lungs are the container." Lungs & Kidneys also involved.
Sinking Qì = Spleen / organ prolapseAspect of Qì Deficiency. Primary organ: Spleen (Zhōng Qì). Clinical result: organ prolapse (uterus, rectum, stomach).
Empty Heat hallmarksNight sweats + heat in 5 palms + low-grade afternoon/evening fever = Yīn deficiency / Empty Heat. NOT excess heat.
Pitting vs Non-Pitting edemaPitting = Yáng deficiency (SP/LU/KD). Non-pitting = Qì stagnation or Dampness.
Blood Stasis = fixed stabbing painQì Stagnation = moving, distending. Blood Stasis = fixed, stabbing/boring. Key clinical differentiator.
False Shén = terminal signSudden dramatic improvement in dying patient = Yīn & Yáng separating. Do NOT mistake for recovery.
Zōng Qì → speech & voiceStored in chest from Air + Gǔ Qì. Weak voice = Zōng Qì deficiency. Also governs breathing strength.
Yuán Qì exits at Source PointsStored at Mìng Mén; distributes via San Jiao; exits at Source Points (Yuán Xué 原穴). Tonifying source points = tonifying Yuán Qì.
Sū Wèn cycles: 7 ♀ / 8 ♂Every multiple of 7 (female) or 8 (male) years marks a new Jīng-governed developmental phase. Key for constitutional assessment.
