Jīng (essence) = candle wax — the foundation. Qì = the flame — energy in action. Shén = the light — most immaterial. "Without the candle you can't have the flame, and without the flame you can't have the light." Jīng is called the ultimate treasure.

⚡
Eight Principles · Bā Gāng Biàn Zhèng 八綱辨證
FCM Ch. 30 · CAM Ch. 10 · Foundation of all pattern differentiation
🎯
The Big Picture The Eight Principles are four pairs of opposites that form the framework for ALL pattern diagnosis in TCM. They tell you the location (Interior/Exterior), temperature (Hot/Cold), relative strength of pathogen vs. righteous Qi (Full/Empty), and the master summary (Yin/Yang). Every other system — Zang-Fu, Six Meridians, Four Levels — uses these as the foundation.
📖
Historical Context (from lecture) Rooted in Han classics — the Suwen and Shang Han Lun. The actual term "Eight Principles" dates to the Qing dynasty (1644–1912) and is based on Zhang Jingyue's Jingyue Quanshu, though greatly simplified from his original description.

🏠 Interior / Exterior

  • Location of disease
  • Stage of disease
  • Direction of travel

🌡️ Hot / Cold

  • Nature of the pathology
  • Yang vs. Yin nature
  • Treatment direction

⚖️ Full / Empty

  • Pathogen vs. Zheng Qi
  • Relative strength
  • Reinforce vs. Reduce

☯️ Yin / Yang

  • Master summarizing pair
  • Cold/Interior/Empty = Yin
  • Hot/Exterior/Full = Yang
⚠️
Key Rule — Location, Not Cause! Differentiation of Interior and Exterior is based on the location of the disease, NOT the etiology. A disease may be caused by an exterior pathogenic factor, but if it is now affecting the internal organs → it is classified as Interior. The cause doesn't matter; where it IS now matters.
🏠
Interior – Exterior Patterns
Location, stage, and direction of disease travel

Exterior Patterns

Located in the superficial portions of the body: skin, hair, muscles, interspaces between muscles, and superficial meridians/collateral channels. Represents the early stage of an exogenous disease.

🩺
Two Types of Exterior Conditions (from lecture) (1) Affect skin & muscles — caused by EPF with acute onset (Wind-Cold, Wind-Heat). (2) Affect channels — slower onset, e.g., Bi Syndrome/Painful Obstruction, characterized by obstruction to Qi circulation in channels & joints by Cold, Damp, Wind, or Heat.

🧠
Prof. Schrier's Memory Hook — Cold Physiology Cold = contracting. So: (1) Cold contracts the pulse → tight quality. (2) Cold closes pores → no sweating. (3) Cold makes you NOT thirsty. (4) Cold stiffens the neck and body → severe aches. When you see tight + no sweat + no thirst → think Cold!

Interior Patterns

Location is the five Zang and six Fu organs. Defines the stage of disease and its direction of travel. Chronic in nature; often mid-to-late stage.

🎮
Prof. Schrier's Monopoly Analogy Some pathogens bypass the exterior stage entirely — like going directly to jail in Monopoly: "Do not pass Go, do not collect $200." The three direct-entry zones are the Stomach, Intestines, and Uterus. They also enter internally via emotions, diet, and overwork.

Disease Transmission

⬇️ Exterior → Interior (Worsening)

  • Aversion to cold → aversion to heat
  • Thin white coat → thick yellow coat
  • Superficial pulse → deep pulse
  • Causes: weak body resistance, hyperactive pathogen, improper/delayed treatment
  • "Just a cold, I'll push through" = lets pathogen dive deeper

⬆️ Interior → Exterior (Improvement)

  • Pulse becomes less deep
  • Tongue coat thins
  • Causes: correct treatment, good care, strengthened resistance
  • Pathogen being expelled toward surface

Full vs. Empty Cold & Heat

❄️ Full Cold (Excess Yin)

  • Pain worse with pressure
  • Abdominal pain aggravated by pressure
  • Pulse: Full-Tight-Deep
  • Tongue: thick white coat
  • Face: bright-white
  • Cause: cold foods, external cold → interior

❄️ Empty Cold (Yang Deficiency)

  • Pain better with pressure/warmth
  • Listlessness; dull-pale face
  • Pulse: Weak-Slow-Deep
  • Tongue: thin white coat
  • Cause: overwork, cold foods, excess sex, internal cold

🔥 Full Heat (Excess Yang)

  • Whole face red; high fever all day
  • Strong thirst for cold water
  • Pulse: Full-Rapid-Overflowing
  • Tongue: red, yellow coat
  • Cause: emotional problems, diet (spicy/alcohol), EPF → interior

🔥 Empty Heat (Yin Deficiency)

  • Malar flush only; heat afternoon/evening
  • Night sweats; dry mouth — drinks in small sips
  • Five-palm heat; insomnia
  • Pulse: Floating-Empty-Rapid
  • Tongue: red, peeled / no coat
  • Cause: overwork, irregular eating, excess sex, blood loss

⚠️
False Heat/Cold ≠ Empty Heat/Cold! Empty Heat and Empty Cold arise from deficiency of Yin/Yang — there IS real heat or cold present. In False Heat/Cold, the appearance is false — there is NO actual heat or cold. This is the most confusing distinction in the Eight Principles!

Treatment Principles

❄️ Cold Treatment

  • Exterior cold: Dispel cold; reducing method; cupping
  • Interior cold: Warm it; reinforcing method; warming needle + moxa
  • Excess cold: Patient feels cold, doesn't like touch → dispel; reducing + cupping
  • Deficiency cold: Patient feels cold but likes touch/warmth → warm; reinforcing + moxa

🔥 Heat Treatment

  • Exterior heat: Expel heat; Jinyinhua, Liangqiao, Banlangen
  • Interior heat: Clear heat; Shigao
  • Excess heat: Patient feels hot → expel; reducing method
  • Deficiency heat: Tonify Yin + clear heat; reinforcing method

Exterior Full vs. Exterior Empty

Full Exterior (Excess)

  • Fever ✓; no sweating
  • Severe body aches
  • Strong aversion to cold
  • Floating-Tight pulse
  • Thin white coat

Empty Exterior (Deficient)

  • Slight or no fever; sweating present
  • Slight body aches
  • Aversion to wind (not cold)
  • Floating-Slow pulse
  • Thin white coat
🔑
The Sweating Key In exterior patterns: no sweating = Full (Excess); sweating = Empty (Deficient). The pores are either locked shut by the pathogen (Excess) or the Wei Qi is too weak to hold them closed (Deficiency). This is the #1 differentiator between Exterior Full and Exterior Empty!

Yin – Yang Patterns + Collapse
The master summarizing pair

Excess Yang (Full Heat)

  • 3 types: (1) External Yang EPF (Wind-Heat, Summer-Heat); (2) Internally generated (Heart-Fire, Liver-Fire); (3) Transformed from other pathogens (Cold → Interior Heat)
  • Restlessness, headache, irritability
  • Pain worse with pressure
  • Red tongue; Full pulse

Excess Yin (Full Cold)

  • Excess Cold consumes Yang
  • Sources: Wind-Cold, Cold-Damp, Cold penetrating directly into Stomach/Intestines/Uterus, Bi syndrome, internally generated Damp/Phlegm
  • Cold limbs, weakness, contracture
  • Pain improved with heat
  • Pale tongue; slow pulse

Deficiency Yang (Empty Cold)

  • Yang spontaneously deficient → apparent Yin excess
  • Feeling cold, desires warmth, cold limbs, pale urine
  • Fatigue, weakness; pain improves with heat AND pressure
  • Pale tongue; slow weak pulse
  • Cause: cold foods, overwork, excess sex, Full Cold

Deficiency Yin (Empty Heat)

  • Yin depleted → apparent Yang excess; consumes Body Fluids, Essence, Blood
  • Main cause: overwork ("burning the candle at both ends")
  • Dryness (mouth, skin, eyes, afternoon); night sweats; 5-palm heat; insomnia
  • Red tongue, no coat; thin rapid pulse

Three Treasures + Jīng 精 / Essence
FCM Ch. 31 · The foundation of all vital substances
🕯️
Prof. Schrier's Candle Analogy (lecture) Jīng = the candle wax and wick — the foundation. Without it, nothing burns. Once it's melted away (depleted), the flame dies. Qì = the flame — energy in action, the Jīng in motion. Shén = the light given off — the most immaterial; the purpose of the candle. You can't have great light without both the candle AND the flame.

Three Types of Jīng

Pre-Heaven Essence

  • From blending of parents' sexual energies before birth
  • Nourishes embryo/fetus
  • Determines constitutional make-up, strength, vitality
  • Fixed in quality AND quantity
  • Goal: preserve by good work, rest, diet, exercise
  • Like an initial bank account — can't add to it directly

Post-Heaven Essence

  • From refined substances from food & fluids by Spleen & Stomach after birth
  • Can be stored and replenished
  • Herbs, food, breathing, exercise all nourish this
  • "Adding money to the bank account through work"

Kidney Essence

  • Combination of Pre + Post Heaven Essence
  • Fixed in quality and quantity
  • Replenished by Post-Heaven Essence
  • Produces Tian Gui (menses in women, sperm in men)

Functions of Essence (Jīng)

5 Key Functions

  • 1. Growth, reproduction, development (cycles of 7 & 8 — Suwen Ch.1)
  • 2. Basis of Kidney Qi
  • 3. Produces Marrow → fills spinal cord → brain
  • 4. Basis of constitutional strength
  • 5. Basis for the Three Treasures (Jing, Qi, Shen)

Jīng Deficiency Signs

  • Children: poor bone development, late fontanelle closure, mental retardation
  • Adults: soft bones, weak knees/legs, low backache, poor memory, loose teeth, hair loss/premature gray, sexual weakness, infertility, tinnitus, deafness, absent-mindedness

6 Functions of Qi

1. 🚂 Transporting

  • Ascending, descending, entering, exiting
  • Movement = no pain; no movement = pain
  • Linked to transformation function

2. 🔥 Warming

  • Function of Yang Qi
  • All physiological processes need warmth
  • Fluids (Yin) need Yang warmth to transform, transport, excrete

3. 🛡️ Protecting

  • Primarily Wèi Qi function
  • Nourishes Còu Lǐ (space between skin & muscles)
  • Linked to Lungs (skin = most exterior)

4. 🤲 Holding

  • Yang Qi holds Yin (fluids/blood) in place
  • Prevents leaking: urinary incontinence, bleeding, prolapse

5. 🔄 Transforming

  • Food → Gǔ Qi → Zhēn Qi
  • Dense forms transformed into subtle forms
  • Jin Ye into clear/thin (jin) and turbid (ye) parts

6. ⬆️ Rising

  • Holds body structures in proper place
  • Weak Qi → sinks → prolapse
  • Different from Holding: Rising = up; Holding = don't leak out

Xuè 血 / Blood
Dense, material form of Qi · Yin in nature
📖
Suwen 26: "Blood is the mind of a person." Ling Shu 32: "When blood is harmonized, the mind is calm in its residence." Blood anchors the Shen. Blood deficiency → insomnia, anxiety, poor memory.

Sources of Blood

OrganRole
SpleenMain source — generates Blood from Gǔ Qi (Food Qi)
LungPushes Gǔ Qi to Heart for transformation
HeartTransforms Gǔ Qi into Blood; governs Blood and circulation
KidneyMakes Blood through Marrow and Kidney Essence

Functions of Blood

1. Nourishes Body

  • All tissues and organs depend on Blood for nourishment

2. Circulates with Yíng Qi

  • Blood and Nutritive Qi move together in vessels

3. Moistens Body

  • Keeps tissues supple and lubricated

4. Houses the Mind

  • Anchors Shén; Blood deficiency → insomnia, anxiety

5. Menstruation

  • Determines menstrual cycle; Liver key role

Organ Relationships

  • Heart: governs + circulates
  • Spleen: makes + holds in vessels
  • Liver: stores + regulates menstruation; nourishes sinews, eyes

🔑
Qi Stagnation vs. Blood Stasis (KEY EXAM COMPARISON) Qi Stagnation: distension > pain; moving pain; wiry pulse; tongue normal/slightly red sides. Blood Stasis: pain > distension; FIXED, stabbing/boring pain; choppy/wiry/firm pulse; PURPLE tongue. Prof. Schrier's gravy analogy: heat condenses blood like thickening gravy on stove. Cold makes ice cubes (congeals it).

Causes of Blood Stasis

Main Causes

  • 1. Qi Stagnation (Qi fails to move Blood)
  • 2. Qi Deficiency (too weak to move Blood)
  • 3. Heat in Blood (condenses/thickens it)
  • 4. Blood Deficiency (→ Qi def → can't move)
  • 5. Interior Cold (congeals like ice)
  • 6. Dampness & Phlegm (aggravates, not direct cause)
  • 7. Trauma
  • 8. Emotions (suppressed → Qi stagnation → Blood stasis)
  • 9. Diet
  • 10. Lifestyle habits

Blood Stasis by Location

  • Liver: purple nails, dark face, painful periods with dark clots, purple sides of tongue
  • Heart: purple lips, stabbing chest pain, mental restlessness, purple tongue (front sides), distended sublingual veins
  • Lung: oppression in chest, coughing dark blood, purple tongue (front sides/front)
  • Stomach: epigastric pain, vomiting dark blood, dark stools, purple center of tongue
  • Uterus: painful periods, dark clots, amenorrhea, fixed abdominal masses, purple tongue

🔄 Jin Ye Processing Pathway

Food/Drink → Spleen (transforms & separates: clean fluids → Lungs; dirty fluids → Small Intestine) → Lungs spread clean fluids to skin, then downward to Kidneys → Small Intestine separates dirty fluids again: pure → Bladder; impure → Large Intestine → Bladder further transforms: pure fluids → upward → exterior → sweat; impure fluids → downward → urine → Large Intestine → dry stools
Key principle: Yin/Yang are infinitely divisible — even within "impure" fluid, there is a purer and more impure aspect.

2A disease is caused by Wind-Cold but has now moved to affect the Liver. Is it an Exterior or Interior pattern?Interior — because differentiation is based on the location of the disease, not what caused it. Once it's in the Zang-Fu, it's Interior, period.

6Patient has slight fever, pronounced aversion to cold, severe body aches, no sweating, no thirst, floating-tight pulse, thin white coat. What pattern?Exterior Cold (Full)
7Patient has fever, slight aversion to cold, slight sweating, thirst, floating-rapid pulse, tongue red on sides. What pattern?Exterior Heat

10Why do herbs for Exterior patterns tend to be leaves and twigs? Why do Interior herbs tend to be roots and seeds?Law of signatures — light, airy plant parts float upward to the superficial layer. Heavy, dense plant parts sink deep to the interior. (Jinyinhua, Bohe for exterior; roots and heavy medicinals for interior)

21Explain the Three Treasures using Prof. Schrier's candle analogy.Jīng = candle wax/wick (foundation, most dense). Qì = the flame (energy in action). Shén = the light radiated (most immaterial, the purpose). Without Jīng → no Qì → no Shén. Jīng is the "ultimate treasure."
22What are the 3 types of Jīng and what distinguishes them?Pre-Heaven: from parents before birth; fixed in quantity/quality; determines constitution. Post-Heaven: from food/fluids after birth; can be replenished. Kidney Essence: combination of both; fixed but replenished by Post-Heaven.

25Trace the Qi formation pathway from Air and Food to Wei Qi and Ying Qi.Air → Lung → Qīng Qi. Food → Spleen/Stomach → Gǔ Qi. Qīng Qi + Gǔ Qi → Zōng Qi (chest). 26What is Yuán Qi and why is it called the "Motive Force"?Source/Original Qi from Kidneys/Mingmén. It arouses and moves the functional activity of ALL organs. Acts as agent of change to transform Zōng Qi into Zhēn Qi. Uses the Triple Burner as conduit. Exits at Source points.
27What are the 6 functions of Qi?(1) Transporting (ascending/descending/entering/exiting). (2) Warming (Yang function — all physiology needs warmth). (3) Protecting (Wèi Qi; linked to Lung). (4) Holding (Yang holds Yin in place — prevents leaking). (5) Transforming (dense → subtle: food → Gǔ Qi → Zhēn Qi). (6) Rising (holds structures in place; weak Qi → prolapse).
28Distinguish Holding from Rising as functions of Qi.Holding = Yang Qi holds Yin substances (blood, fluids) from leaking OUT (incontinence, bleeding). Rising = Qi holds body structures UP in place (prolapse if deficient). Different directions, same root deficiency.
29What is Qi Stagnation? What is its hallmark symptom and pulse?Qi blocked, not flowing. Most common cause: emotional stress. Hallmark: DISTENSION (more than pain); pain waxes/wanes with emotions. Wiry pulse. Tongue normal or red on sides. Frequent sighing; gloomy feeling; irritability.

Spleen: normal ↑, pathological ↓ → diarrhea/prolapse. Lung: normal ↓, pathological ↑ → cough/asthma. Heart: normal ↓, pathological ↑ → mental restlessness/insomnia.

Yè 液: Yin; turbid/heavy/dense; circulates with Yíng Qi in interior; slow; controlled by Spleen/Kidneys (Middle/Lower Burner); moistens joints/spine/brain/bone marrow.

Part C · Clinical Case Questions

🩺
Prof. Schrier's approach: Step 1 — Exterior or Interior? Step 2 — Hot or Cold? Step 3 — Full or Empty? Then cross off options. These cases follow that same logic.
#Case / QuestionAnswer & Reasoning
C1 Patient: sudden onset 2 days ago. Slight fever, PRONOUNCED aversion to cold. Severe body aches and stiff neck. No sweating. No thirst. Floating-tight pulse. Thin white tongue coat.

What is the pattern? What 2 herbs would you think of?		 Exterior Cold (Full). Reasoning: floating pulse = exterior; tight = cold contracting; no sweat = full; no thirst = cold; white coat = cold.
Herbs: Fangfeng (防風), Xinyihua — light leaves/twigs to expel cold at the surface. Acupuncture: head, arm, upper back + cupping.
C2 Patient started with aversion to cold and fever 3 days ago. Now the aversion to cold is GONE. They have irritability, thirst for cold water, dark urine, and a thick yellow tongue coat. Pulse is now deep and rapid.

What happened? What does this indicate about prognosis?		 Exterior Cold transmitted to Interior Heat. The disappearance of aversion to cold + onset of aversion to heat = the key transition marker. Thick yellow coat + deep pulse = now interior. Thirst + dark urine = heat. Prognosis: worse — pathogen has moved inward, indicating weakened body resistance or improper/delayed treatment.
C3 Patient feels hot in the palms, soles, and chest — but only in the late afternoon. Night sweats. Dry mouth but only drinks in small sips. Dry throat at night. Tongue is red with NO coating. Pulse is floating, empty, and rapid.

What pattern? What is the root cause?		 Empty Heat (Deficiency of Yin). Key signs: heat only in afternoon/evening (not all day = not Full Heat); five-palm heat; night sweats (sweating during Yin time of day); dry mouth with small sips (fluids damaged); Red peeled tongue (Yin consumed, no fluid to make coat); Floating-Empty-Rapid pulse. Root cause: Yin deficiency — most likely from overwork.
C4 Patient has red cheeks and seems feverish. However, the rest of the face is white. They are listless and want to lie curled up in bed. Voice is low and quiet. When asked about thirst, they say they have it — but they want warm fluids, not cold. Urine is pale. Pulse is rapid and floating but feels EMPTY on pressure.

What is happening? Why is this dangerous to misdiagnose?		 True Cold – False Heat (Jiǎ Rè Zhēn Hán). The red cheeks + apparent fever are FALSE — extreme interior Cold is driving Yang to the surface. Clues: rest of face white; wants to be covered; wants WARM fluids; low voice; curled up (Yin behavior); pale urine; pulse rapid/floating but EMPTY (deficient). Dangerous to misdiagnose because treating as Heat (clearing method) would further damage Yang and could cause collapse.
C5 Patient has been having severe, stabbing pain in the lower abdomen — the pain is FIXED in location and worse at night. Menstrual blood is dark with dark clots. She also has purple nails, a dark facial complexion, and purple coloring on the SIDES of the tongue. Pulse is wiry and firm.

What pattern? Which organ? How do you know it's that organ specifically?		 Liver Blood Stasis. Pattern: Blood Stasis (stabbing fixed pain; dark clots; purple tongue; choppy/firm pulse; purple complexion). Organ = Liver because: (1) Liver manifests on the NAILS (purple nails); (2) Liver governs menstruation (dark menstrual blood, clots); (3) Purple on the SIDES of tongue = Liver area; (4) Wiry pulse = Liver. Liver Blood Stasis is distinguished from Blood Stasis elsewhere by these Liver-specific signs.
C6 Patient complains of bloating that moves around and is better after sighing. The bloating worsens when they're stressed at work. They also feel irritable, have frequent mood swings, and sigh a lot. Tongue is normal, pulse is wiry.

What pattern? What is the primary organ? How is this different from Blood Stasis?		 Qi Stagnation (Liver Qi Stagnation). Key: distension/bloating > pain; MOVING pain; comes and goes with emotional state; frequent sighing (Liver's sound); wiry pulse = Liver; irritability. Different from Blood Stasis: Qi Stag has MORE distension than pain; pain MOVES; tongue normal/slightly red sides (not purple); pulse wiry (not firm/choppy). Blood Stasis has FIXED stabbing pain; masses; purple tongue; firm pulse.
