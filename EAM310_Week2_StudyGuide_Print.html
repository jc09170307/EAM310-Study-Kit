🩺 The Floating Pulse Trap — Schrier's Most Important Pearl

Floating + exterior: simultaneous fever & chills, stiff neck, runny nose — pathogen fighting at the surface.
Floating + Yin deficiency: night sweats, five-palm heat, malar flush, afternoon fever, dry throat at night — Yin can't anchor Yang.
Always put a "?" next to floating pulse until you see the full constellation. Cold limbs + hot torso = likely Qi stagnation blocking warmth outward, NOT a Cold pattern.

5bEight Principles — Pattern Manifestations (From Slides)

From Schrier's slides Ch.30 — these four pattern pairs are directly testable.

Full Heat — Excess of Yang

From slides: When excess Heat (from Exterior or Interior) consumes Body Fluids, leading to Dryness or more Heat. Three types of Excess Yang:

  • External Yang pathogenic factor — e.g. Wind-Heat or Summer-Heat
  • Internally generated Heat — from emotional stress or diet, e.g. Heart-Fire, Liver-Fire
  • Transformation from other pathogenic factors — e.g. external Wind-Cold turning into interior Heat

Symptoms: Restlessness, headache, irritability, pain worse with pressure, red tongue, full pulse

Empty Cold — Deficiency of Yang

From slides: Denotes the deficiency of physiological heat (Yang Qi). Cold symptoms: feeling cold, desiring warmth, cold limbs, pale urine. Can occur after an Excess Cold condition has damaged the Yang. Also caused by: excessive consumption of cold foods, excessive physical work/overwork.

Symptoms: Signs of excess cold but arising from a deficiency of Yang — fatigue, weakness, pain which improves with heat and pressure, pale tongue, slow and weak pulse

Full Cold — Excess of Yin

From slides: When excess Cold in the body consumes the Yang (heat). Sources:

  • External Yin pathogenic factor — Wind-Cold or Cold-Dampness
  • External Cold invading Interior directly (bypassing Exterior stage) — Stomach, Intestines, Uterus
  • External Cold invading channels and joints — Painful Obstruction Syndrome (Bi Syndrome)
  • Internally generated Dampness or Phlegm

Symptoms: Cold limbs, weakness, contracture, pain improved with heat, pale tongue, slow pulse

Empty Heat — Deficiency of Yin

From slides: Deficiency of Yin involves the excessive consumption of body fluids, the Yin substances of each organ, the Essence & Blood. Overwork is the main cause. Deficiency of Yin may also be caused by Full-Heat.

  • Yin deficiency signs: Dryness, dry mouth in the afternoon, dry skin, dry eyes
  • Empty Heat signs: Night sweats, heat in the 5 palms, insomnia, red tongue, thin and rapid pulse

🩺 Key Differentiator: Pressure Response

Full patterns (Excess): pain worse with pressure — pathogen resists. Deficiency patterns: pain better with pressure and warmth — emptiness wants support. This applies universally to both Cold and Heat patterns.

Tongue Observation Process (From Slides)
  • How far the tongue protrudes / posture of the tongue (how it sits)
  • Observe Coat: moisture of coat · color of coat
  • Observe Body: moisture of body · color of body
  • Observe Body locations: cracks · coat · color · spots · accumulations & protrusions
  • Color and state of sublingual veins
  • Inspect tip → middle → root. 5–10 seconds max. Relaxed protrusion only.

Methods for Taking Pulse (From Slides)
  • Time: Classically early morning when Yin is calm and Yang has not yet come. Practically: whenever patient arrives.
  • Arm position: Patient's arm horizontal, NOT held higher than the level of the heart
  • Placement: Three fingers (index, middle, ring) simultaneously on the radial artery. Gives initial assessment of strength, level, quality.
  • Arrangement: Spread or press fingers together according to the size of the patient's arm
  • Regulating: Middle finger (longest) slightly contracted — fingertip pads, not tips, contact the artery
  • Using fingers: Ring finger is slightly more sensitive than others — take this into account when comparing position strengths
